Pretzels

Description

You won't believe how easy it is to make homemade pretzels. With this 2-ingredient dough, you can have fresh, warm, and golden soft pretzels on your table in no time. Keep the toppings simple with melted butter and coarse salt, or take things over the top with cinnamon sugar or Parmesan Cheese. A quick bath in a baking soda solution will create the iconic, chewy crust you love from a soft pretzel. -Annie Campbell

With as little ingredients as possible, you'll have some of the softest pretzels you will ever taste, enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons baking soda
  • 1 cup self-rising flour, plus more for kneading
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon coarse salt, or as needed

Steps

  1. Bring 6 cups of water to a low boil in a large pot. Add baking soda
  2. Preheat the oven to 175 degrees celsius. Grease a baking sheet.
  3. Mix 1 cup of flour and Greek yogurt together in a medium bowl until shaggy dough forms. Transfer to a surface dusted with more self-raising flour, and knead for 8 to 10 minutes. Continue adding flour as needed if the dough is too sticky.
  4. Cut the dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll each segment into a long, thin rope, 22 to 24 inches in length. Twist the rope into a pretzel shape by taking the two ends, twisting them around each other, then securing both ends side-by-side onto the center portion of the rope.
  5. One by one, carefully drop each pretzel into the water and boil until the pretzel floats to the top, 2 to 3 minutes. Drain on paper towels or a cooling rack.
  6. Transfer to the preapred baking sheet.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Remove from the oven and increase the oven's temperature to 220 degrees celsius. Brush the pretzels with some of the melted butter, continue to bake until golden brown. 4 to 5 minutes more. Brush with reamining butter and top with coarse salt.
